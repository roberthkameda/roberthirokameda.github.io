4 BRANDING

BRANDING allows designers to let their creative outlets take in charge for most of the work, yet still require a business mindset, since the end-product would have to ultimately focus on the user.

Through the studies at BLOC & my personal experience with all realms of art, I’ve learned that focusing on creating simple and authentic brand logos, color palettes & typographies that synchronises well with the philosophy, theme, and culture of the product (as well as the company), are what makes branding effective and visually appealing.

5 PROTOTYPE

Starting off the PROTOTYPE stage with Low-Fidelity affirms the user’s basic decision making skill when observing and initiating specific actions while on the device. The goal here is to make the visual design of the product as concrete as possible, in order to prevent from creating a clickable prototype that will unfortunately frustrate the user.

From my experience, I noticed that I went through many iterations and changes during the High-Fidelity phase compared to the low fidelity phase, simply because of the endless variations of how a designer can visually edit the contents, texts and images. But I believe, empathizing with the user’s intrinsic needs and focusing on the major components (MVP) of the product are what makes the high-fidelity prototype so effective and attractive.

6 TEST

In the TEST stage, the main goal is to gather as much feedback as possible (both good and bad) in order to modify and/or validate the product. I see this as an indirect and genuine interaction between a random user you’ve never met telling you brutually honest opinions on the designer's IQ (and possibly EQ?). Sometimes it can emotionally hurt designers for their effort, but I see this as an opportunity to go back and grind through the process till the users show gratification.

7 ITERATION

No one’s perfect, and although designers are a breed of human beings that crave perfection in all aspects of life, we’re not perfect as well. This means, iterating the whole project, double-checking whether you've missed anything, and reviewing if something isnt right can save a designer from more trouble when the product is launched, and fortunately prevent receiving bad reviews.

8 VALIDATION

When the level of confidence is high and you’re positively sure that this product went through a substantial amount of ITERATION, its possible to say the designer (the hero we all need), did her/his job, and deserves a drink. *Cheers!*

In my case, at that moment, I feel nothing but satifisfaction. I’m proud of my work and my dedication in solving a stranger’s problem, and that’s what drives me to continue working on the next product, so that the users of that device won’t have to go through the pain anymore that I can genuinely empathize with.
