PONOCRAVE

OVERVIEW

Ponocrave is a mobile app I designed that enables users to locate food trucks near them, pre-order, and pay online.

PROBLEM

San Francisco and Oakland is known for its popular food truck culture, however, an user-centric mobile app for navigating hot food trucks and ordering online are hard to discover. This gave me an opportunity to create a deep analysis and strive for an end-to-end solution for future food-truck connoisseurs.

SOLUTION

After following the guideline of my 8-step design process (Reseach, Goal, Storytelling, Branding, Prototype, Test, Iteration, and Validation), I was able to create Ponocrave, an user-friendly and user-centered food-truck app, suited for food lovers.

4 BRANDING

STYLE GUIDE

The story behind the turtle-shaped food truck is really simple and personal. One, I really love turtles. Two, in my motherland, Hawaii, turtles (honu) are considered as guardian spirits, and are great navigators at finding their home when lost in the sea. I simply wanted users to feel like they’re at home when using Ponocrave. Three, the shape of the reptile looks like a food-truck. The shell repesents the exterior design of the kitchen (inside) where the chefs cook, and the rest of the body resembles the remaining aspect of an automobile vehicle (ex. wheels). Nevertheless, I ultimately wanted to create a logo that was playful, welcoming, & delightful to see.

My reasoning behind the naming of Ponocrave is simple as well. Living the pono way of life in Hawaii means, living in conscious decision to do the right thing for the self, others, and the environment. As a designer, I wanted to create a food-truck app that provides the user with a list of only the healthiest, non-gmo, & organic food suppliers, and I thought using the two colors that resemble our mother nature (ocean & forest) can teach and bring awareness to users on appreciating nature and follow a more natural & sustainable way of life.

I chose Avenir as my main typography, because of its modern & crisp tone that transcends to our eyes. Why would anyone want a font that would scare away users? I rather choose a font thats welcoming & soulful. Hope you like it as well.

Throughout the whole LO-FI phase, my goal was to simply focus only on laying out the basic architecture of each page on Ponocrave. The purpose of LO-FI was to make sure the user was able to visually identify and execute the correct commands without the designer’s assistance. What really helped me during this process was to imagine the user as a toddler or a senior citizen, who had little experience with digital devices.

HI-FI

During the HI-FI phase, my goal was to visually encrypt the design I made during the LO-FI phase, and polish it up with circular shapes and patterns that resembles warmth, sustainability, and naturalism. Colors of mother nature were implemented, but it also embodied the essence of a modern and technological movement, in my sense. My end goal was to incorporate a simple & minimal theme of design into Ponocrave, yet also have a playful and soft approach for new users.

6 TESTING

USABILITY TESTING

In order to achieve user satisfaction, I observed the performance of each interviewee for both, the low-fidelity prototype using Balsamiq & the high-fidelity prototype using Invision. For most of the duration, I chose not to interfere, allowing them figure out how to complete the objectives I instructed prior to the test. This gave me clarification on how user-friendly and user-centric Ponocrave was, and allowed me to take notes on what attributes to keep and edit.

USER FEEDBACK

Visual-related remarks and the simplistic qualities of many attributes of the app (ex. login / sign up) were highly noted by all three candidates. I also believe the color variation or scheme really did go well with the culture of Ponocrave, and the candidates seem to find the overall color scheme to be visually appealing and easy on the eyes. On the contrary, I was glad to gather negative feedbacks, where one of the candidates had a hard time adding and subtracting the quantites of the selected meal, due to his large fingers. This was an easy problem to solve, considering the solution was to simply enlargen the plus/minus sign for a large person's finger to fit.

7 ITERATION & 8 VALIDATION

After few iterations, and re-editing some of the issues that were identified in the previous stage, I felt that it was appropriate to submit my final work, believing that the app encompasses my philosophy and idea of what a “GREAT” food truck app should possess, what the user’s need (MVP) in a food truck mobile app, and several secondary features that allows them to ful-fill their overall experience.
