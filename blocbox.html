BLOCBOX

OVERVIEW

Blocbox is a could-based software I designed that allows users to save, share and collaborate information: create simple notes, store images, and add links.

PROBLEM

User’s found it frustrating to use current software services, currently active in the market, that allows them to organize & manage all kinds of information. Based on the intervieews, it was clear that current products weren’t user-focused and didnt offer an seamless user experience, and my role was to fix this.

SOLUTION

After following the guideline of my 8-step design process (Reseach, Goal, Storytelling, Branding, Prototype, Test, Iteration, and Validation), I was able to create Blocbox, an Saas product, that let users share and collect information with ease, and also include qualities that distinguishes from other typical book-marking features.

4 BRANDING

STYLE GUIDE

During the making of blocbox’s logo, typography, and color palette, I thought implementing the themes of minimalism & simplicity was a perfect fit for the brand’s reputation and culture. I wanted to create a professional brand, yet also include a tint of playfulness and a welcoming vibe.

Focusing on the theme of minimalism and simplicity, I thought including only the letter B inside a blue box, would be perfect. My goal at this point was to keep the tone of the brand logo, somewhat neutral at most, and let the content of blocbox do the talking.

The complimentary and opaque flavor of the color blue to me represents the essence of calmness. I simply wanted users to use blocbox with ease and gentle flow throughout the whole process.

6 TESTING

USABILITY TESTING

For Blocbox's case study, I used Peek & Invision to test out how the users reacted to the overall architecture and design of the web page. During the tests, Peek's audio and video recording of the users change in tone (voice), various cursor movements, and overall behavioral patterns, gave me susbtantial knowledge on what the user liked and disliked. Each test gave me a clarification of what aspects of the website to keep, and fix the ones to prevent frustrations.

USER FEEDBACK

1. All three users mentioned the overall design layout and the color coordination was neat and professional. The buttons lightened up the screen which allowed them to easily navigate to the next step of the process.

2. One of the recomendations from the users, was to create a video tutorial or even an enlarged cursor that pointed the user to the correct direction or button, preventing any kind of collision or frustration he/she may have.

7 ITERATION & 8 VALIDATION

The iteration process was the last step before I was ready to confirm that the product was ready to launch and go live. Gathering the data I collected from the previous stage, TESTING, I realized some parts of the website had to be edited.

1. A great example that I had to go back and forth a couple times, was figuring out if the users felt more comfortable with a horizontal or vertical dashboard. Although both layouts seemed simple and neat, it came down to how humans find it more comfortable when informations are layed out vertically, simply due to the eye structure and its simple movements. Once those issues were solved to the best of my ability, the product was ready to be launched.
