ABOUT
My name is Hiro, born in Honolulu, Oahu, raised in Tokyo, Japan for most of my life, and I’m a recent graduate from The University of California, Berkeley with a Bachelors in English Literature. As an English Lit major, I was trained to observe the world, absorb all realms of behavior and emotion the characters express, and holistically analyze the author’s concept and contextual vision of the society we live in. My passion for English Lit (and inevitably Design) comes from my utmost respect for art, humanity, and technology. Having the privelege of connecting with other humans from previous, present, and future generations through literature and words, taught me one vital skill I’ll deeply cherish as a designer and writer; empathy. During my last semester at Cal, I stumbled upon the UI Design Showcase held at the Jacobs Institute of Design towards the end of my college life, and I can genuinely say at that moment I fell in love with both the functionality and creative aspect of today’s digital interface shared between humans and machines. It was a eureka moment, that this was the career path I wanted to devote & pursue, regardless of my inexperience in design, which imminently led to my relation with BLOC. Not knowing a single thing about UX/UI Design, I dove into an unknown oceanic territory, and at times I felt utterly lost in translation, but what kept me through was the guiding current of my mentors at BLOC, my obsessive passion for interweaving the functionality and creative aspects of UX/UI, reaping out the wisdom of other professional & experienced designers on LinkedIn, reading design-focused books & articles, and attending design conferences in the Bay Area. As a designer, I’ve learned to obsessively revolve around the user, empathize with their needs as the utmost priority, and ultimately deliver a user-focused product. During my studies at Bloc, it was vital to follow my design process I made while working on projects, which I believe can echo through all experienced designers and creative thinkers.
