ABOUT

My name is Hiro, born in Honolulu, Oahu, raised in Tokyo, Japan for most of my life, and I’m a recent graduate from The University of California, Berkeley with a Bachelors in English Literature. As an English Lit major, I was trained to observe our world, absorb all realms of behavior and emotion the characters (humans) express, and holistically analyze the author’s conceptual and contextual vision of the society we live in.

My passion for English Lit (and inevitably Design) comes from my utmost respect for art, humanity, and technology. Having the privelege of connecting with other (priveleged & unpriveleged) humans from previous, present, and future generations through literature and words, taught me one vital skill I’ll deeply cherish as a designer and writer; empathy. If you'd like to see an ongoing collection of my all-time favorite fiction/non-fiction books click here.

What made me strive to become a Designer? Two stories. My first one happened to be during my last semester at Cal, when I stumbled upon the UI Design Showcase held at the Jacobs Institute of Design towards the end of my college life, and I can genuinely say at that moment I fell in love with both the functionality and the creative aspect of today’s digital interface shared between humans and machines. It was a epithanic realization, that this was the life path I wanted to devote & pursue for the rest of my life as a human, regardless of my inexperience in design, which imminently led to my relation with BLOC.

The other one was back in Japan, during August of 1996, where I veritically fell from my four-story apartment and severely damaged my left leg. Retrospectively, it was a critical turning point in my life that taught me how to embrace my pain and frustration, and use it as an advantage. Luckily I survived, and an amputation of my left leg wasn't necessary, but the emotional and mental pain was incurable during my younger years. As I grew older, and now wiser, I learned there were more people like me out there, who were also finding a cure to make their life less stress-free and happier. As I approached towards my last few months of college, it just made so much sense to pursue a career as a designer, merely due to its human-centered, empathetic approach in curing all types of pain, frustration, and anger.

Not knowing a single thing about UX/UI Design, I dove into an unknown oceanic territory, and at times I felt utterly lost in translation, but what kept me through was the guiding current of my mentors at BLOC, my obsessive passion for interweaving the functionality and creative aspects of UX/UI, reaping out the wisdom of other professional & experienced designers on LinkedIn, reading design-focused books & articles, and attending design conferences in the Bay Area.

As a designer, I’ve learned to obsessively revolve around the user, empathize with their needs as the utmost priority, and ultimately deliver a user-focused product. During my studies at Bloc, it was vital to follow my design process I made while working on projects, which I believe can echo through all experienced designers and creative thinkers.
